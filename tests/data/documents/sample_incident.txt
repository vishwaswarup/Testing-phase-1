Sri Lanka Easter bombings — April 21, 2019

On Easter Sunday, April 21, 2019, a series of coordinated suicide bombings struck
three churches and three luxury hotels in Colombo, Negombo, and Batticaloa,
Sri Lanka.

The attacks killed 269 people and injured more than 500 others, making it the
deadliest act of violence in Sri Lanka since the end of the civil war.

The National Thowheed Jamath, a local militant group, was responsible for the
bombings. Islamic State later claimed responsibility for the coordinated attack.

The bombers used explosive vests and vehicle-borne improvised explosive devices.
The targets included St. Anthony's Church, St. Sebastian's Church, and Zion Church,
as well as the Shangri-La Hotel, the Cinnamon Grand Hotel, and the Kingsbury Hotel.

The Sri Lankan government declared a state of emergency following the attacks.
